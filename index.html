My childhood story isn't one for the books. There isn't much to tell about it. For one, the most interesting part of my childhood would be that I graduated kinder as a valedictorian. It was great, and I did a whole speech in front of so many people, but it wasn't as remarkable as people may think. I was kinder, after all, and what I achieved then is incomparable with the achievements of other people in the levels that actually matter, such as right now in college. I was just a quiet kid then. I didn't have so much friends growing up, and those few friends I had are so distant now. I didn't have that "iPad kid" era during my childhood, due to the fact that I didn't have access to gadgets early in life. I had my first tablet at around 8 or 9, and even then I had restrictions, which did help to avoid the addiction towards gadgets during that time. I studied at a catholic school, which meant constant prayers before each class, and having to go through monthly masses and angelus every noon. Back then, I wasn't allowed to have my own money, and had to bring lunch everyday to school, which did make me jealous of my classmates who could buy at the canteen. I did eventually get the freedom to buy using my own allowance, sometime around Grade 5 onwards. I wasn't fond of pop culture, mainly due to not having access to the internet and not being exposed to current trends. Until now I'm still not a fan of the latest... everything. I remember vividly that I auditioned for the school church choir and when asked to sing anything I didn't know any popular songs so I had to sing a church song, which isn't bad considering I was auditioning for a church choir, but in that moment I felt a bit ashamed for not knowing what others could easily sing at that moment. Looking back, it's easy to understand why I wasn't the most social as a kid. I wasn't able to keep up with others, and that led to them not being fond of me. It's mainly the reason why I was quiet and didn't have much of that "exciting" and "fun" childhood everyone brags of.
